Information Security Policy

This policy defines Acme Corp's approach to protecting the information assets of the company, its employees, customers, and partners. All employees, contractors, and third parties with access to Acme systems must comply with this policy. Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract.

This policy is owned by the Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) and is reviewed annually, or following any material security incident. The current version was approved on 2026-01-15.

Data Classification

All data handled by Acme employees must be classified according to the following scheme. Classification determines how data may be stored, shared, and disposed of. When in doubt, classify at the higher level.

Public Information

Information that is approved for unrestricted external distribution. Examples:

  • Marketing materials and press releases
  • Published product documentation and API references
  • Job postings and recruitment materials
  • Content published on acme.com or our public GitHub repositories

Public information may be shared freely. No special handling is required, though accuracy and brand compliance must still be maintained.

Internal Use Only

Information intended for Acme employees and approved contractors only. Sharing outside the company without explicit approval from a department head is prohibited. Examples:

  • Internal process documentation and this handbook
  • Roadmaps and OKRs (non-financial)
  • Engineering architecture diagrams
  • Aggregated, anonymised product usage metrics

Internal information must be stored in company-approved systems (Google Workspace, Confluence, GitHub Enterprise). Storing on personal devices or unapproved cloud storage is not permitted.

Confidential and Restricted

Sensitive information whose exposure could harm the company, its customers, or employees. Examples:

  • Customer PII (names, email addresses, payment information)
  • Employee salary and performance data
  • Source code for unreleased products
  • M&A and financial planning data
  • Cryptographic keys, credentials, and secrets

Confidential data must be encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+). Access is granted on a need-to-know basis only. Sharing requires written approval from the data owner and must be logged.

Access Control

Access to Acme systems follows the principle of least privilege: every user, service, and system is granted only the minimum access required to perform its function. Access rights are reviewed quarterly by system owners. Upon a role change or departure, IT Security revokes access within 24 hours; the off-boarding checklist in Workday triggers automatic de-provisioning for Microsoft 365, Slack, GitHub Enterprise, and Google Workspace.

Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA is mandatory for all employee accounts, including:

  • Microsoft 365 (Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive)
  • Google Workspace (email, Drive, Calendar)
  • GitHub Enterprise
  • Workday (HR and payroll data)
  • Slack
  • AWS, GCP, and Azure cloud consoles
  • VPN access
  • Any system tagged as "Confidential" in the asset register

Approved MFA methods are hardware security keys (YubiKey — preferred), authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy), and SMS as a last resort. SMS OTP may not be used for systems with access to Confidential data.

Lost or compromised MFA factors must be reported to IT Security within 1 hour. IT Security will revoke the affected sessions and issue replacement tokens.

Password Policy

Passwords for company systems must meet the following requirements:

  • Minimum 16 characters
  • No repetition of the previous 10 passwords
  • Must not appear in known breach databases (checked automatically at creation)
  • Mandatory rotation every 180 days for privileged accounts
  • No mandatory rotation for non-privileged accounts (NCSC guidance)

Use of a password manager (1Password, which Acme provides) is required for all accounts. Storing passwords in plain text, spreadsheets, or code is strictly prohibited and is a disciplinary matter.

Privileged Access Management

Production database access, root or admin roles in cloud environments, and access to cryptographic key material are classified as privileged access. All privileged access must be:

  • Just-in-time (requested, approved, and time-limited via Teleport)
  • Logged to an immutable audit trail
  • Approved by a second engineer for production data access
  • Reviewed and confirmed by the CISO quarterly

Acceptable Use Policy

Acme provides computing resources and network access for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted as long as it does not interfere with work, consume excessive resources, or expose the company to legal or reputational risk.

The following activities are strictly prohibited on company-owned devices and networks:

  • Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content
  • Mining cryptocurrency
  • Running unapproved software or shadow IT services
  • Connecting unapproved external storage or devices via USB
  • Disabling or circumventing endpoint security software

Working from public Wi-Fi networks without VPN is not permitted when accessing Internal or Confidential systems. Use the Acme VPN (Tailscale) at all times on untrusted networks.

Incident Response

A security incident is any event that may compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of Acme systems or data. This includes suspected incidents — do not wait for confirmation before reporting.

Reporting an Incident

Report suspected security incidents immediately via:

  • Slack: post in #security-incidents and tag @security-oncall
  • Email: security@acme.com (monitored 24/7)
  • Phone: +44 20 7946 0000 (out-of-hours security line)

Do not attempt to investigate or contain the incident yourself before reporting. Do not delete any logs or evidence. Preserve the state of affected systems where possible and await guidance from the security team.

Severity Levels

Incidents are categorised as follows:

  • P1 — Critical: active exfiltration, ransomware, or widespread service compromise. Response within 15 minutes, 24/7. Executive notification within 1 hour.
  • P2 — High: suspected breach of Confidential data, compromised privileged account. Response within 1 hour during business hours, within 2 hours out-of-hours.
  • P3 — Medium: suspicious access patterns, failed privileged access attempts, phishing confirmed. Response within 4 business hours.
  • P4 — Low: policy violation, lost device (low data risk), spam campaign. Response within 1 business day.

Post-Incident Review

Every P1 and P2 incident requires a written post-incident review (PIR) within 72 hours of resolution. The PIR documents the timeline, root cause, impact, containment actions, and preventive measures. PIRs are shared internally and archived for audit purposes. They are blameless: the goal is systemic improvement, not individual accountability.
