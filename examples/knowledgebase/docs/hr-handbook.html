Welcome to Acme Corp

Acme Corp is a technology company built on the belief that great software comes from great teams. This handbook explains what we offer, what we expect, and how we work together. It is a living document — if something is unclear or out of date, open a pull request against the HR repository.

All employees are expected to read this handbook within their first two weeks. Questions can be directed to people@acme.com or raised in the #people-questions Slack channel.

Leave Policy

We offer a generous and flexible leave policy because we believe rest is productive. Taking leave is not just permitted; it is encouraged. Managers are responsible for ensuring their teams take meaningful time off throughout the year.

Annual Leave

All full-time employees receive 25 days of annual leave per calendar year, in addition to public holidays. Leave accrues from day one and may be taken in any increment of at least half a day.

  • Up to 5 unused days may be carried over to the following year
  • Days beyond the 5-day carry-over limit are paid out at year-end
  • Leave requests of 5 or more consecutive days require 4 weeks' notice
  • Leave requests of fewer than 5 days require 2 business days' notice

Book leave in Workday. Your manager will approve or decline within 2 business days. If a request is declined, the manager must explain why and suggest an alternative window. Once approved, the leave period is automatically blocked in your Microsoft 365 calendar and an out-of-office reply is configured for your Outlook mailbox.

Sick Leave

Sick leave is separate from annual leave and has no fixed limit. If you are unwell, do not come to the office — notify your manager before your working day begins or as soon as you are able.

For absences of 1 to 3 days, self-certification is sufficient. For absences of 4 or more consecutive days, a doctor's certificate is required. Sick leave is recorded in Workday by your manager and does not affect your performance review.

Parental Leave

We offer 26 weeks of fully paid parental leave to all new parents, regardless of gender or the method by which they became a parent (birth, adoption, or surrogacy). Leave may begin up to 4 weeks before the expected due or placement date.

  • Primary carers: up to 26 weeks fully paid
  • Secondary carers: up to 6 weeks fully paid, with an option to extend using the shared parental leave allowance
  • Both parents may take leave simultaneously during the first 4 weeks

Parental leave does not affect pension contributions, leave accrual, or performance review eligibility. Return dates may be adjusted by mutual agreement.

Compassionate Leave

Up to 5 days of paid compassionate leave is available for bereavement of an immediate family member (partner, parent, sibling, child). Additional unpaid leave may be requested. For extended or complex situations, speak to HR directly.

Benefits and Perks

Our benefits package is designed to support your health, financial security, and professional growth. Benefits are available from day one of employment.

Health Insurance

Acme provides private health insurance through Bupa, covering:

  • GP and specialist consultations
  • In-patient and day-patient treatment
  • Mental health support (up to 10 sessions per year with a psychologist)
  • Dental and optical add-on available at employee's cost via salary sacrifice

Cover extends to a spouse or civil partner at a subsidised rate (50 % of premium). Enrolment is handled through the Bupa online portal; HR sends the link in your first week.

Pension and Retirement

Acme contributes 6 % of your basic salary into a defined-contribution pension plan through Scottish Widows. Employee contributions may be made via salary sacrifice, reducing National Insurance liability.

  • Minimum employee contribution: 3 % (auto-enrolled)
  • Employer match: 6 % regardless of employee contribution level
  • Additional voluntary contributions: up to the annual allowance (£60,000 for 2025/26)

Learning and Development

Every employee receives an annual learning budget of £2,000 for professional development. This may be spent on:

  • Conferences and workshops (travel and accommodation included within budget)
  • Online courses, books, and subscriptions
  • Professional certifications (exam fee and one retake included)

Spend must be approved by your manager in advance. Submit receipts in Workday within 30 days of the expense. Unused budget does not carry over and is not paid out.

Other Perks

Additional perks include a monthly £50 wellbeing allowance (gym, fitness classes, meditation apps), free lunch in the London office Monday to Thursday, a cycle-to-work scheme, and a company-wide hackathon one Friday per quarter.

Performance Reviews

We run a biannual performance review cycle: mid-year check-in in July and an annual review in January. The process is designed to be forward-looking — career development and goal-setting take priority over retrospective grading.

Review Cycle

The full review cycle runs over 6 weeks:

  • Weeks 1–2: Self-assessment submitted in Lattice
  • Weeks 2–3: Peer feedback collected (3 peers nominated by employee, 1 by manager)
  • Weeks 3–4: Manager writes their assessment
  • Week 5: Calibration session across the leadership team via Microsoft Teams
  • Week 6: Review conversation between manager and employee; meeting invite sent via Outlook

Review outcomes and compensation changes are recorded in Workday at the close of Week 6. Employees receive a summary notification by email via Microsoft 365.

Self-Assessment

The self-assessment covers four areas:

  • Impact: What did you deliver, and how did it affect the company?
  • Growth: What did you learn and how did you develop this cycle?
  • Collaboration: How did you contribute to your team and peers?
  • Goals for next cycle: What do you want to achieve and how will you get there?

Aim for 300–500 words per area. Be specific: cite projects, outcomes, and evidence. Avoid generalisations. The self-assessment is the foundation of your review conversation.

Ratings and Compensation

Performance ratings use a five-point scale: Exceptional, Exceeds Expectations, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement, and Unsatisfactory. Ratings feed into the annual compensation review in February, where base salary adjustments and equity refresh grants are determined.
