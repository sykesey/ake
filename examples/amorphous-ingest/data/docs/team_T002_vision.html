Product Design Team Vision 2025

Team ID: T002 | Department: Design | Lead: Casey Liu (E006) | Headcount: 3

Mission

We create cohesive, accessible, and delightful experiences that make our product feel like a single, thoughtful system. The Design team owns the design language, interaction patterns, and the research process that grounds all product decisions in real user needs.

Design System v2 — The Year's Defining Investment

Our most significant investment in 2025 is the Design System v2 (PR002). The current system has grown organically over three years and now has 34 documented inconsistencies in component behaviour across web and mobile surfaces. Version 2 will be component-library-first, token-driven, and built for dark mode from the ground up. All colour, spacing, and typography decisions will be expressed as design tokens that propagate automatically across platforms.

Goals for 2025

  • Ship Design System v2 with 40 core components by end of Q2 — Casey Liu (E006) leading
  • Complete Onboarding Redesign (PR004) — already delivered Q1, reducing time-to-value by 38%
  • Establish a user research cadence: one moderated study per two-week sprint
  • Deliver WCAG AA accessibility audit and remediation plan by Q3
  • Partner with Core Platform on Enterprise Portal (PR006) UX — handoff target 2025-05-15

Research Agenda

Avery Park (E007) will lead a discovery study in May to understand how enterprise administrators use the current admin interface, informing the Enterprise Portal design. Quinn Hassan (E008) owns the accessibility audit programme, beginning with the top 10 most-used screens.

Team Capacity and Prioritisation

With three designers serving the full product surface, prioritisation is our biggest challenge. We are introducing a design request triage process in Q2 to give partner teams clearer SLAs and reduce context-switching costs on the team. Incoming requests will be categorised as: immediate (one sprint), planned (backlog), or deferred (not this half).

The Design System v2 work accounts for approximately 60% of Casey Liu's time and 40% of Avery Park's time through June. New design requests during this period will be evaluated against this constraint.

Success Metrics

  • Design System v2 adoption: 80% of new UI code using v2 components by Q4
  • Onboarding completion rate: target 72% (up from 54% baseline)
  • Design-to-engineering handoff cycle time: target 3 days (down from 8 days)
